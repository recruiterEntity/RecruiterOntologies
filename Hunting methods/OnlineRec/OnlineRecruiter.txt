Here's a basic outline for an "Online Recruiting Ontology" based on the provided information:

1. **Entities:**
   - JobOpening
   - JobBoard
   - Candidate
   - SocialMediaPlatform
   - IndustryGroup

2. **Relationships:**
   - PostedOn (relating JobOpening to JobBoard)
   - SearchedOn (relating Candidate to JobBoard)
   - ConnectedOn (relating Candidate to SocialMediaPlatform)
   - BelongsToIndustryGroup (relating Candidate to IndustryGroup)

3. **Attributes:**
   - JobTitle
   - JobDescription
   - CandidateName
   - CandidateProfile
   - IndustryInterest

4. **Job Application Process:**
   - Define the process and stages involved in job application.
   - Track the status of job applications.

5. **Recruitment Channels:**
   - Specify different job boards and their specialties.
   - Include parameters for search features on job boards.

6. **Social Media Engagement:**
   - Capture interactions on social media platforms.
   - Record connection requests, messages, and shared content.

7. **Networking Groups:**
   - Categorize and define industry-specific groups.
   - Track participation and engagement in these groups.

Remember, this is a basic structure, and you can customize it based on your specific requirements and the intricacies of online recruiting in your organization. Adjust the ontology to suit the nuances of your recruiting processes and the platforms you utilize. If you have further questions or need more details, feel free to ask!
